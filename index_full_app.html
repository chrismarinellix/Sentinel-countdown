What is Project Sentinel?

Project Sentinel is our response to a highly competitive engineering talent market. Our biggest challenge isn't technical capability - it's retaining exceptional people in an industry where engineers constantly receive offers from competitors. We're in a talent war, and traditional responses like higher salaries and retention bonuses create an expensive arms race we can't win on cost alone. Project Sentinel creates competitive advantage through culture, development opportunities, and meaningful work - factors that money alone can't buy.

This program combines project management excellence with a quality assurance framework that embeds dedicated process guardians within each regional team. Sentinel team members dedicate 5-10% of their time to ensuring every project follows established procedures, completes required checklists, and maintains quality standards.

üíº The Business Lens - What You Look At

üîÑ How Work Flows Through the Business

  • Handoffs: When work passes from one person/team to another - are they smooth or messy?
  • Bottlenecks: Where does work pile up? Why? Who's waiting on whom?
  • Dead zones: Where does work get lost or forgotten?
  • Delays: What causes projects to slow down or stall?
  • Rework: Why are we doing the same thing twice? What went wrong the first time?

  • Waiting: Delays waiting for approvals or responses

  • Outputs: What we deliver (reports, designs, models) - are they consistently professional and accurate?
  • Record keeping: Can we find what we need? Is it up to date? Is it accessible?
  • Standards: Does work meet our quality bar regardless of who did it or which region?

üéØ What We're Actually Doing

Reducing Friction

Every process should flow smoothly. We identify and remove friction points - those small annoyances that slow people down, cause errors, or create frustration. Sometimes it's as simple as reorganizing a checklist or automating a single calculation.

Minimal Change, Maximum Impact

We believe in doing less, better. Instead of overhauling entire systems, we make targeted, strategic improvements. A 10-minute process change that saves 2 hours per project is worth far more than a complex solution that takes weeks to implement.

Simple Things, Big Difference

Often, the simplest improvements create the most value. A template instead of starting from scratch. A dropdown instead of manual entry. A reminder instead of forgotten steps. These small changes compound into massive efficiency gains.

‚úÖ

Improving Communication

‚úÖ

Making Good Work Easier

üë• Sentinel Adjudication Meetings

The Sentinel team gathers weekly to review submissions and make decisions:

Weekly Meeting Structure

  • Frequency: Every week during the competition quarter
  • Duration: 30-60 minutes depending on submission volume

  • Decision Making: Majority vote required; ties go to submitter's benefit
  • Documentation: All decisions logged in audit trail with rationale
  • Feedback: Submitters notified of decision within 48 hours
